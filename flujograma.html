Phase 3
Foreclosure Action — Steps / Process
Type of Foreclosure
⚖️ JUDICIAL

Foreclosure Complaint Filed in Court

  • Complaint · Summons · Lis Pendens
  • Service of Process

Service of Process

  • Personal service
  • Substitute service
  • By publication (if not located)

Borrower Response Period

  • A. No response (Default)
  • B. Borrower files an answer
CONTESTED

Litigation / Motions / Discovery

  • Motions to dismiss · Appeals
  • Discovery requests · Mediation
  • Settlement conferences
  • Loan modification negotiations

Motion for Summary Judgment or Trial

  • Court grants summary judgment or case goes to trial

Judgment of Foreclosure and Sale Entered

  • Court order allows property to be sold
CONTESTED MATTER RESOLVED
⏱ Judicial Timeline Uncontested: 6–12 months
Average: 12–24 months
Highly contested: 2–10+ years
📑 NON-JUDICIAL

Reinstatement / Cure Period

  • Borrower pays past-due payments
  • Late fees & legal/trustee costs
  • Pre-sale reinstatement period

Notice of Trustee Sale (NOTS)

  • Recorded · Mailed to borrower
  • Posted on the property
  • Published in a newspaper
  • Sets the auction date
⏱ Non-Judicial Timeline Notice of Default: 1–3 months
Notice of Sale to auction: 3–4 weeks
Trustee deed/transfer: 1–2 weeks
Total: 4–8 months

Phase 4
Foreclosure Sale

Redemption Period (some states)

  • Court allows borrower to Reinstate or Pay in Full (PIF)

⚠️ BANKRUPTCY — HOLD/STOP

Borrowers often file BK before/on day of Foreclosure Sale to delay.

Types: Chapter 7 (Liquidation) · Chapter 13 (Pay-back) · Chapter 11 (Reorganization)

Scenarios:
A. BK proceeds normally
B. BK dismissed → stay lifted → returns to Judgment
C. Multiple BK filings → stay not imposed → FC continues
Judicial Path

Foreclosure Auction

  • Property auctioned to highest bidder
  • Lender takes title if no bidder

Sale Confirmation Period (some states)

  • Court reviews and confirms the sale

Transfer of Title / Eviction

  • Deed recorded to winning bidder
  • If borrower stays → eviction proceedings
Non-Judicial Path

Foreclosure Auction / Trustee Sale

  • Sold at public auction
  • At courthouse, county steps, or online

Trustee Deed Issued

  • Trustee's Deed Upon Sale issued to winner
